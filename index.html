How to auto-edit videos to music in DaVinci Resolve

Three steps. Zero manual cutting. Your edit synced perfectly to every beat.

1

Detect beats in your music

Select your audio track and click Detect. Pulse Edit uses AI-powered beat analysis to find every beat, bar, and subdivision. Adjust sensitivity to catch only strong beats or every nuance. Supports any time signature: 2/4, 3/4, 4/4, 5/4, 6/4, 7/4.

2

Auto-edit clips to beats

Choose a cut pattern (Energy Map, Every Beat, Buildup, Wedding, and 7 more). Pulse Edit automatically takes clips from your Media Pool, cuts them to length, and places them on the timeline perfectly synced to every beat. Sequential or random clip order, with transitions.

3

Apply effects with AI mood matching

Choose a mood (Calm, Energetic, Epic...) or manually apply speed ramps with Optical Flow, zoom effects with 14 easing curves, and freeze frames. Each clip gets unique, non-repetitive effects matched to the music's energy.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I auto-edit videos to the beat in DaVinci Resolve?

Pulse Edit detects beats in your music, then automatically cuts and places clips from your Media Pool to the timeline synced to every beat. Choose from 11 cut patterns including Energy Map. Works inside DaVinci Resolve Studio 18 or later.

What is the best BeatEdit alternative for DaVinci Resolve?

Pulse Edit is a BeatEdit alternative that goes beyond markers. While BeatEdit only places beat markers ($49.99), Pulse Edit auto-edits your clips, applies speed ramps with Optical Flow, zoom effects with 14 easing curves, AI mood analysis, and freeze frames — all synced to the beat.

Do I need DaVinci Resolve Studio?

Yes. Both Pulse Edit and Beat Markers require DaVinci Resolve Studio 18 or later (the scripting API is only available in Studio). The free version of Resolve is not supported.

Can I sync video cuts to music automatically?

Yes. Pulse Edit analyzes the beats in your music track, then automatically cuts and places video clips onto the timeline synced to those beats. You can choose from 11 cut patterns to control the rhythm and pace of your edit.

Is this a subscription?

No. One-time payment, lifetime license. You buy it once and use it forever, including free updates. No monthly fees, no annual renewals.

What's the difference between Beat Markers and Pulse Edit?

Beat Markers only places markers on your timeline. Pulse Edit does that plus auto-edits your clips to beats, applies speed ramps, zoom effects, AI mood matching with 6 presets, and freeze frames. Pulse Edit includes everything Beat Markers does and much more.

How does the AI Mood Analysis work?

Choose a mood preset (Calm, Happy, Melancholic, Energetic, Aggressive, Epic) and adjust the intensity. Pulse Edit automatically selects the right speed ramp types, zoom curves, easing styles, and effect distribution for each clip based on the music.

What is Optical Flow in speed ramps?

Optical Flow is DaVinci Resolve's advanced motion estimation for retiming. When Pulse Edit applies a speed ramp, it enables Optical Flow automatically so slow motion looks smooth and cinematic instead of choppy or stuttering.

What platforms are supported?

macOS with Apple Silicon (M1, M2, M3, M4). Windows support is planned for a future release. Requires DaVinci Resolve Studio 18 or later.

AutoCut costs $6.60–$14.90 per month (subscription) and focuses on silence removal. Pulse Edit is a one-time purchase (€79.99) with beat detection, 11 auto-edit cut patterns, speed ramps, zoom effects, and AI mood analysis. No monthly fees, no annual renewals.

Can I use this for wedding videos, travel montages, and event recaps?

Yes. Pulse Edit includes a "Wedding / Slow" cut pattern for elegant edits, plus Energy Map for high-energy event recaps and travel montages. Speed ramps and zoom effects add cinematic flair to any project — weddings, reels, music videos, commercials, and social content.

Resolve's native "Show Music Beats" only shows visual beat indicators on the waveform — you still have to cut manually. Pulse Edit and Beat Markers place real, snappable markers on the timeline, and Pulse Edit goes further by auto-editing clips to beats with effects.
