FAQ

Do I need DaVinci Resolve Studio?

Yes. Both Pulse Edit and Beat Markers require DaVinci Resolve Studio 18 or later (the scripting API is only available in Studio). The free version of Resolve is not supported.

What platforms are supported?

macOS with Apple Silicon (M1, M2, M3, M4). Windows support is planned for a future release.

Is there a free trial?

Yes. Beat Markers has 3 free uses, Pulse Edit has 4 free uses. No credit card required. Full functionality during the trial.

Is this a subscription?

No. One-time payment, lifetime license. You buy it once and use it forever, including free updates.

What's the difference between Beat Markers and Pulse Edit?

Beat Markers only places markers on your timeline. Pulse Edit does that plus auto-edits your clips, applies speed ramps, zoom effects, and AI mood matching. Pulse Edit includes everything Beat Markers does and much more.

Can I upgrade from Beat Markers to Pulse Edit?

Yes. Use discount code UPGRADE20 at checkout to get €20 off Pulse Edit. You effectively pay the difference.

How does the AI Mood Analysis work?

Choose a mood preset (Calm, Happy, Melancholic, Energetic, Aggressive, Epic) and adjust the intensity. Pulse Edit automatically selects the right speed ramp types, zoom curves, easing styles, and effect distribution for each clip.

What is Optical Flow?

Optical Flow is DaVinci Resolve's advanced motion estimation for retiming. When Pulse Edit applies a speed ramp, it enables Optical Flow so slow motion looks smooth instead of choppy.
