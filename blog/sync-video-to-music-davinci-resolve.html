How to Sync Video to Music Beats in DaVinci Resolve (2026 Guide)

By Abramo Benedetti — March 18, 2026 — 5 min read

Editing video to the beat of music is one of the most satisfying things in post-production. A perfectly timed cut on a kick drum, a speed ramp hitting the bass drop, a zoom synced to a snare — it's what separates amateur edits from professional ones.

But doing it manually? It's tedious. You're scrubbing through a waveform, tapping markers, nudging clips frame by frame. For a 3-minute music video, that's 180+ cuts done by hand.

In this guide, I'll show you 3 ways to sync video to music in DaVinci Resolve, from fully manual to fully automatic.

Method 1: Manual Beat Cutting (Free, Slow)

This is what most editors start with. You listen to the music, tap a key to mark each beat, then cut your clips at each marker.

Steps:

  1. Import your music into the timeline
  2. Play the audio and press M on every beat to place a marker
  3. Manually cut each video clip at the marker positions
  4. Trim and adjust each clip to fill the gaps

Pros: Free, complete control over every cut.
Cons: Extremely time-consuming. A 3-minute song with 4/4 timing has ~180 beats. That's 180 manual cuts, plus trimming. For a single reel, you're looking at 30-60 minutes of repetitive work.

Method 2: DaVinci Resolve's Built-in "Show Music Beats" (Free, Limited)

DaVinci Resolve 18+ has a built-in beat detection feature. Right-click your audio clip on the timeline, then select Analyze Audio Beat > Mark Beats.

What it does:

  • Shows visual beat indicators on the waveform
  • Helps you see where beats land

What it doesn't do:

  • Place real, snappable markers on the timeline
  • Auto-cut or auto-place clips
  • Apply any effects or speed ramps

It's better than nothing, but you still have to cut everything manually. The beat indicators are visual only — your clips don't snap to them, and there's no way to auto-edit based on them.

Method 3: Automatic Beat Detection + Auto-Edit (Plugin)

This is where dedicated plugins come in. Instead of placing markers by hand, the software analyzes your audio, detects every beat, and either places markers or auto-edits your clips to the beat.
